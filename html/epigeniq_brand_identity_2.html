04 — Brand Voice
Precise
Not vague
Every claim is sourced. Nobel science. NIH funding. 30M scans. Numbers over adjectives. Data over inspiration.
Provocative
Not preachy
Ask the uncomfortable question. "What if your supplements aren't absorbing?" Reframe before you educate.
Intelligent
Not academic
Translate the science into plain language without dumbing it down. Treat the audience like they're smart and curious.
Confident
Not arrogant
State the thesis clearly. Don't hedge with "maybe" or "might." The science is real. Own it.
Warm
Not clinical
This is about human longevity. Real people. Real biology. The warmth is in the mission, not the marketing.
Early
Not trendy
EpiGenIQ was paying attention before this was mainstream. That credibility compounds. Never chase the moment.
05 — Usage Guidelines
✓ Do
  • Always capitalize as EpiGenIQ (never Epigeniq or EPIGENIQ)
  • Use the ™ symbol on first mention in every document
  • Lead with education before invitation
  • Always attribute science claims to their source
  • Pair the icon mark with the wordmark on first use
  • Use Spectral cyan as the primary CTA color
  • Include HealthStartz parent attribution in footer
  • Use "active scanning device" not "wearable"
  • Include Nu Skin compliance disclaimer on commercial content
✕ Don't
  • Never use the logo on a busy photographic background
  • Never stretch or distort the icon mark proportions
  • Never call Prysm iO a "wearable" or "medical device"
  • Never make income claims in public-facing content
  • Never use Nu Skin name/logo without company approval
  • Never use purple gradients (generic AI aesthetic)
  • Never write in all caps for body text
  • Never promise specific health outcomes
  • Never use the brand for TikTok opportunity promotion
